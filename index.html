Attributes

Attributes are rated from 0-10. To determine your starting attribute, roll 2d6-2 for each one.

Attributes are mostly meant to determine what your character is like and for adjudicating certain situations (roll dexterity to pick locks for example).

  • Strength
    • Examples: Smash door, climb tree
  • Agility:
    • Examples: Balance, sneak
  • Dexterity:
    • Examples: Picking a lock, picking a pocket
  • Intelligence
    • Examples: Decipher script, identify magic item, identify potion
  • Charisma
    • Examples: Diplomacy, Bluffing
  • Wisdom:
    • Examples: Binding a wound
  • Luck:
    • Examples: Secret

Attributes do not change your attacks, saving throws, AC, etc.

Maneuvers

Due to your weak tie to Venrik, you occasionally are seized with divine inspiration. When not stressed due to battle or other extenuating circumstances, choose 5 of the maneuvers you know. Those maneuvers are prepared for you.

Management of maneuvers is simulated by use of playing cards. Each card represents one of the maneuvers you have prepared. At the beginning of your first turn of the encounter draw three (3) cards at random from the 5 maneuvers you've prepared. At the beginning of your following turns, draw an additional card from the maneuvers you haven't drawn yet. When you use a maneuver, place the card in your discard pile. If you have no more maneuvers you can draw, discard your current hand of cards and shuffle it into your discard pile. That pile then becomes the new pool you can draw from. Immediately draw two cards.

At higher levels you will know more maneuvers than you can prepare. To change your prepared maneuvers, you can spend 10 minutes meditating and praying to Venrik.

When you gain a level in this class, you can choose a maneuver you know and replace it with a different maneuver of the same level.

There are 3 kinds of maneuvers: Strikes, Counters and Boosts.

Unless otherwise specified, strikes are a standard action to use and involve you making a melee standard attack. Counters are always a reaction action. Unless otherwise specified, boosts are swift actions.

Enlightened Focus

As a disiciple of the enlightened, you have gained the ability to bring order to the chaotic elements of your soul. By meditating for 10 minutes, you can remove the chaos from your system.

This state of balance lasts until it is disrupted. Roll a d6 at the beginning of each turn in a stressful situation like combat. If you roll a 4, 5, or 6, your balance is disrupted. By disrupting it, you achieve a temporary state of enlightenment, walking in the footsteps of those that came before you — Gentle Breeze and Kruglauk.

While in this state of enlightenment, you gain the following benefits:

  • You partially ignore the tragedy of flesh — You take half damage from all sources.
  • Your mind is shielded from the influence of others — You automatically succeed on any saving throws against fear, rage, or other mental effects such as mind control.
  • Your blows strike swift and true — You can make a melee standard attack as a free action each round.

These benefits last until your are unconscious, the battle is over or 10 minutes pass, whichever comes soonest.

Unarmed and Unarmored

While wearing no armor, your base AC is higher than 10. The value is given in your class table. You are deftly able to dodge blows due to your ability to perceive reality in a different way.

Additionally, while wearing no armor your speed is increased by 10.

You are deadly with the fist. Your unarmed strikes deal extra damage. Additionally, if you take the rapid attack action and only attack with unarmed strikes, you ignore the normal penalties from the action.

You do not need to be unarmed to make unarmed attacks.

Disciple Discoveries

As each Disciple of the enlightened makes their way in the world, they discover new fragments of enlightenment. Choose a discovery from the list below.

  • Empty Body
    • You have discovered how to very briefly not be somewhere and then immediately reappear. It is so brief that it requires great reflexes to achive, but is very useful when trying to avoid a dragon's breath. When you make a reflex saving throw to take half damage, you take no damage on a succesful saving throw and half damage on a failed saving throw.
  • Abundant Step
    • You have discovered that movement is an illusion, as all is one. This discovery is hard to wrap your mind around, but allows you to appear wherever you desire, as there is no "where". You can appear at any "location" you can see instantaneously as a free action up to "600 feet away". You cannot bring anything with you except the clothes on your back and what you hold in your hands. Once you have done this, you will need to meditate as even after you perform it you form pearls of doubt. You can't do this again until you complete your 10 minute meditation where you remove chaos from your system.
  • Purity of Body
      You have discovered that hunger and thirst are illusions. You no longer need to eat or drink and cannot be diseased or poisoned.
  • The Thickness of Air
      You have discovered that air is as thick as we choose to perceive it to be. You can use this fact to slow and cushion your movements. You take no damage from being thrown or falling.
  • Leap of the Clouds
      Your jump distance is no longer limited by your speed, height or size.
  • Uncanny Awareness
      You have discovered the presence of a vestigial, ethereal third eye that we all have. You are never surprised or flat-footed.
  • Deflection
      You can deflect arrows as a reaction, taking no damage from the one missile. As a full-round action, you can deflect all missiles that would strike you until the beginning of your next turn.
  • The Laws of Momentum
      You can use an enemy's momentum against them. Whenever an enemy attacks you but misses, you make make a standard melee attack against them as a reaction.
  • Tongue of the Sun and Moon
      You have discovered the proto-language of all humanoids. You can speak to any humanoids via it, although the concepts you can communicate are limited to basic ones.

Maneuvers

The Dragon Knights of Bahamut train vigorously and are rewarded with special powers. Outside of battle, choose to ready a number of maneuvers as given by your class table. You cannot ready the same maneuver more than once. When a battle starts, you have immediate access to the maneuvers you have readied. At higher levels you will know more maneuvers than you can prepare. To change your prepared maneuvers, you can spend 10 minutes meditating.

Alchemical Aim

The Master of the Five Humors can add his level to attack rolls and difficulty classes of alchemical items he uses (not his concoctions).

Chirugeon

If tending to wounds, the Master of the Five Humors adds his level to the amount healed. If treating a creature that is diseased or poisoned, the creature can add the level of the Master of the Five Humors to his fortitude save.

Concoctions

The Master of the Five Humors understand the needed balance of the five essential humors: Blood, Black Bile, Yellow Bile, Phlegm and Occult Bile. When these humors are out of balance, illness and other tragedy ensue. However, he also knows how to manipulate these humors for temporary positive effects. It is dangerous to manipulate a person's humors too frequently. They can do so to a single creature once per hour and each concoction lasts for 1 minute.

The master can create his concoctions by spending an hour. Each concoction takes a slot. He can create as many of each as he wants.

Injecting a concoction into a creature is a standard action. If the creature is unwilling, they are allowed a DC 15 reflex save to avoid. Only the master can inject these concoctions, everyone else is too dumb. Injection is a melee move.

Choose concoctions from the list below:

  • Concoction of Valor — 5 Temp HP and +1 saves against fear
  • Concoction of Speed — +10 movement speed and +1 reflex saves
  • Concoction of Fire — Can belch a 3d6 15' cone of fire at a target once as a standard action. DC 15 reflex for half.
  • Concoction of Speed — -5 HP and can make a free standard attack once per round.
  • Concoction of Strength — +2 to melee attacks and +2 damage.
  • Concoction of Precision — +2 to ranged attacks and +2 damage.
  • Concoction of Insanity — Berserk— +4 attack and damage, 50% resistance to damage, attacks until there are no creature within sight, targets randomly (includes allies).
  • Concoction of Shrink — Shrinks to halfling size (melee attacks deal 50% damage and are small).
  • Concoction of Light — Glow Brightly, 30 ft radius.
  • Concoction of Frog — Turn into a giant frog.

Additionally, investing skill points have certain other effects, as described below:

  • Climb Wall — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in climb, you can climb at full speed instead of reduced speed.
  • Disarm Trap — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in disarm trap, you automatically find traps on chests.
  • Escape — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in escape, your movements do not provoke attacks of opportunity.
  • Fast Talk — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in fast talk, your first failed lie is ignored.
  • Improvised Attacks — You can add your ranks in this skill to non-conventional attack rolls or difficulties. For example, if you throw pocket sand in someone's eyes, that is normally a DC 10 reflex save to avoid. If you had 8 ranks in this skill, it would be DC 18 instead.
  • Pick Lock — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in pick lock, you can freely lock and unlock any lock you have succesfully picked.
  • Pick Pocket — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in Pick Pocket, you can pick pocket things secured to them, like necklaces or sheathed swords.
  • Sneak — If you have invested at least 3 skill points in sneak, you can achieve basic success without rolling. An example of basic success is creeping down a hallway undetected to see what is up ahead.

Weapon Talents

Weaponmasters can pick weapon talents they qualify for from the lists below. Whenever a weaponmaster gains a level in this class, they can retrain a talent to a talent of the same level that they qualify for. Additionally, each level they gain a new talent, which they can pick from any talent they qualify for.

Level 1 Talents

  • Dual-Weapon Fighting — You can dual-wield without penalties and gain a +1 bonus to AC while dual-wielding.
  • Great-Weapon Fighting — You add double your strength bonus to damage with two-handed melee weapons instead of single your strength bonus.
  • Small-weapon Fighting — You add double your strength bonus to damage with one-handed melee weapons instead of single your strength bonus when you fight with one one-handed melee weapon.
  • Ranged-weapon Fighting — You do not suffer penalties for firing into a melee.
  • Shield Fighting — You receive +1 AC when fighting with a shield. Additionally, if you would be reduced to less than 1 HP, your shield is destroyed and the damage is reduced by the hp increment of the shield * how many notches of durability it had remaining. This can only happen once per encounter.
  • Cross-bow Fighting — You receive a +2 bonus to attacks with crossbows and may add your dexterity bonus to the damage.
  • Bow Fighting — When shooting your bow, you may choose to suffer a -2 penalty on all of your attacks for the round. If you do, you can make an extra attack with the bow. Your attacks this round must not be special attacks.
  • Axe-fighting — If you drop an enemy with an axe, you immediately get a free attack with it. Additionally, you have +1 attack when fighting with axes.
  • Sword-fighting — If you are using a sword, you get double the benefit of fighting defensively. Additionally, you have +1 attack when fighting with swords.
  • Hammer-fighting — Your critical hits with hammers also knock the target down. Additionally, you have +1 attack when fighting with hammers.
  • Spear-fighting — You do not suffer a dead-zone when fighting with spears. Additionally, you have +1 attack when fighting with spears.
  • Flail-fighting — Your attacks with flails ignore an enemy's shield bonus to AC. Additionally, you have +1 attack when fighting with flails.
  • Unarmed-fighting — Your unarmed attacks deal 1d6 damage, deal lethal damage and do not provoke attacks of opportunity. Additionally, you have +1 attack when fighting unarmed and +1 to grapple checks. Unarmed attacks may be dual-wielded.
  • Master Tripper — You get a +4 bonus to trip attempts. Additionally, if you succeed on your trip attempt you may immediately make an extra melee attack on the victim.
  • Master Grappler — You get a +4 bonus to grapple attempts and grapple checks. Additionally, if you succeed on your grapple attempt, you may immediately deal grapple damage.
  • Master Disarmer — You get a +4 bonus to disarm attempts. Additionally, if you have a free hand or can free your hand as a free action, you may have the item you disarmed end up in your hand instead of on the ground.
  • Master Sunderer — You get a +4 bonus to sunder attempts. Additionally, if you succeed on your sunder attempt you may immediately make an extra melee attack on the victim.
  • Master Bull — You get a +4 bonus to bull rush and overrun attempts. Additionally, if you succeed on your overrun attempt, you can kick them for free.
  • Cleave — When you drop an enemy with a melee attack, you may make an extra standard attack as a free action.
  • Combat Reflexes — You may make an additional reaction each round.
  • Dodge — +1 dodge bonus to AC
  • Diehard — If you would be reduced to < 1 HP, you are instead reduced to 1 hp. This may happen at most once per encounter.
  • Quick-draw — You can draw/stow items as a free-action instead of a standard action when drawing or stowing additional items each round.
  • Run — You run at x5 speed instead of x4 speed and suffer no defensive penalties for doing so.
  • Punisher — You receive a +4 bonus to attacks of opportunity and deal +1d6 damage if you hit with them.

Elvish Accuracy

Elves ignore the penalty for shooting into melees and can precisely fire into grapples without any risk of attacking anyone but who they intended. Additionally, they ignore 20% concealment.

Arcane Attack

Periodically elves discover the ability to combine magic with war.

Each time you gain this feature, choose one of the special attacks below. You can use this attack once per encounter. You regain the use of them by trancing for 5 minutes.

The ability is activated as a swift action and affects the next attack made. If used with a melee attack, the effect does not discharge until you actually hit an enemy or the battle is over.

  • Shocking Attack — Add 1d6/level lightning damage to an attack.
  • Translocating Attack — Choose one— You are teleported to where your attack lands. If it struck a creature, you appear next to them; OR A creature or object of medium size or smaller that you strike with this attack is teleported next to you. For example, if you make a called shot to an ogre's club, its club appears next to you.
  • True Strike — Add +10 to your attack roll and ignore less than total cover and concealment. This effect discharges even if you miss.
  • Acid Attack — Add 2d4 acid damage to your attack. The victim continues to take 2d4 acid damage each round for as many rounds as you have levels.
  • Force Blanket — A foe struck by this attack deals half damage with their next succesful attack as the force blanket absorbs much of the impact.
  • Hidden Attack — A creature or object of medium size or smaller that you strike with this attack becomes invisible for as many rounds as you have levels.
  • Twin Strike — Your weapon is in two places at once. You can attack two targets instead of one with this attack. This effect discharges even if you miss.

Equipment

Encumbrance

  • A character can carry 15 slots of items without being encumbered.
  • They receive a -1 penalty to attack and AC for each slot beyond 15 they are carrying.
  • Two day's worth of food is 1 slot.
  • Weapons are 1 slot, except for two-handed weapons which are 2 slots.

Durability

  • Items have 3 "notches" of durability.
  • When an item is out of notches, it is destroyed or broken.
  • A weapon gets -1 penalty to attack for each notch missing.
  • Armor gets a -1 penalty to AC for each notch missing.
  • Notches are usually lost from sundering, but the DM may rule that certain effects like fireballs can damage weapons too if the user fails a save.
